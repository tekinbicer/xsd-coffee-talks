Gas-hydrate formation experiments at 2-BM

Posted at — Jan 20, 2020

Abstract

We present the results of dynamic in-situ 3D X-ray imaging of methane hydrates microstructure during methane hydrate formation in sand and coal samples by using an environmental cell for studying geomaterials and available at the 2-BM beamline. Short scanning times, high resolution and phase contrast provided by synchrotron X-rays allowed for better understanding of water movement and different types of gas-hydrate formation. Complementing previous observations, we conclude that the process of gas-hydrate formation is accompanied by the water movements caused by cryogenic water suction that happens in sequences of short fast movements with longer equilibrium states in between. Based on the 3D microstructure we identified two types of gas-hydrate formation: (i) into the gas pockets and (ii) inside water volumes. The rate of dissociation in the self-preservation mode (pressure drop at negative temperatures) appears to be different for these two types of gas hydrates. This means that the history of the gas-hydrate formation may influence its behaviour at the dissociation stage (e.g. gas-hydrate production). We also demonstrate our new chamber for simultaneous dynamic imaging and measuring acoustic properties of the samples during the methane-hydrate formation. These combined measurements are useful for further developing methods of acoustic well-logging data interpretation.

Bio

Viktor Nikitin is currently a postdoc at Max IV Laboratory and a consultant at the XSD group of APS. Viktor completed his Ph.D. in Mathematics at Lund University in 2016, his Bachelor and Master degrees are both in IT. Viktor’s main research interests include inverse problems, high-performance computing, and practical experiments. He recently developed fast algorithms for time-resolved tomography data reconstruction and 3D ptycho-tomography reconstruction. He also conducted several tomographic experiments at the 2-BM beamline.
