Phase Imaging beyond the Diffraction Limit with Electron Ptychography

Posted at — Sep 26, 2019

Abstract

Aberration-corrected optics have made scanning transmission electron microscopy a widespread and often essential tool for characterization at atomic scale. Traditionally, image resolution is dominated by beam energy and the numerical aperture of the lens, with state-of-the-art reaching ~0.47 angstrom at 300 keV. Here, by combining a new electron microscope pixel array detector with high dynamic range and ptychography to recover phase information from the full phase space, we achieved a spatial resolution at more than double the diffraction limit of the lens, and improved the dose efficiency compared with conventional imaging technique, simultaneously providing a four times faster acquisition, double the information limit and double the precision. I will present recent developments in ptychographic reconstruction techniques, including generalized maximum likelihood and machine learning approaches, and demonstrate how they further push the limit of electron ptychography by overcoming experimental challenges such as scan noise, probe variation, and multiple scattering.

Bio

Dr. Yi Jiang is a postdoctoral appointee in the microscopy group at the Advanced Photon Source. He received his Ph.D. in Physics from Cornell University and B.S. in Physics from University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. His research focuses on developing image reconstruction and processing algorithms for X-ray/electron tomography and ptychography experiments.
