An overview of super-resolution technique for X-ray imaging

Posted at — Jul 29, 2019

Abstract

In the past, image scale-up/super-resolution task was completed by making use of either interpolation method or the sparse coding based dictionary learning method. However, in the recent times, the popularity of machine learning tools such as neural networks to solve a wide range of imaging problems meant that even the super-resolution problem was resolved using the Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs). In this talk, we will present some of the popular CNN-architectures that are relevant to scale-up the X-ray images. The efficacy of these CNN based super resolution approaches will be evaluated with the aid of quantitative metrics such as the Fourier Ring Correlation, the Peak signal-to-noise ratio (PSNR) and the Structural Similarity Index (SSIM).
