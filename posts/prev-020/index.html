Hard X-ray ptychographic imaging with broadband illumination

Posted at — Oct 7, 2019

Abstract

As a multi-measurement scanning coherent diffraction imaging (CDI) method, X-ray ptychography is aimed to realize high spatial-resolution X-ray imaging beyond the resolution limit set by X-ray optics. A fundamental issue is that the high spatial resolution requires that the sample be illuminated by a high dose scale which is approximately inverse fourth power of the resolution, thus making higher fluxes very desirable to push the resolving power of CDI techniques. One way to increase the illumination flux is using a double-multilayer monochromator (DMM) optics with about 0.8% bandwidth. Compared with our double-crystal monochromator (DCM) optics with 0.01% bandwidth, this DMM optics provides around 20 times more flux. However, in this case the increased flux comes as a cost of degraded coherence, which conflicts with the coherent illumination prerequisite of the conventional formulations of CDI and ptychography. In this presentation, I will introduce the multi-wavelength reconstruction method that has been implemented to deal with the resulting degraded temporal coherence from such an illumination to ensure high-quality reconstruction.

Bio

Yudong Yao is a postdoctoral appointee in Microscopy Group at Advanced Photon Source, Argonne National Laboratory.
